Rosalind Franklin

The scientist who discovered DNA's double helix.

Here is a timeline of Rosalind Franklin's life:

  • 1920 - Franklin was born July 25th 1920 in Notting Hill, London, England
  • 1941 - She graduated from Newham College with a degree in Natural Sciences
  • 1945 - Franklin obtained her PhD from Cambridge
  • 1951 - She made her first breakthrough about the properties of DNA which lead to further experiments
  • 1952 - The famous 'Photo 51' that confirms the double helix structure of DNA is taken in Franklin's laboratory
  • 1953 - Watson and Crick are shown Photo 51 and use it to publish their conculsions about DNA's structure. No credit is given to Rosalind Franklin.
  • 1958 - Rosalind Franklin passed away from complications of cancer.
